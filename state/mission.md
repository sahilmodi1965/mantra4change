# Mission

## Self-description (verbatim)

- **Mission:** "Enabling education leaders to drive sustainable and continuous improvements across 1,50,000 schools by 2025."
- **Vision:** "Every child receives an enriching learning experience."
- **Self-framing:** Bangalore-based non-profit working in school improvement and education leadership development. Founded 2013 by Khushboo Awasthi and Santosh More.

Source: [mantra4change.org](https://www.mantra4change.org/), [mantra4change.org/about-us](https://www.mantra4change.org/about-us/)

## Theory of change (three pillars)

> **Inspire:** We nurture the education ecosystem to amplify the focus on school improvement by enabling civil society organisations through innovative programs, knowledge sharing of resources, and collective action.

> **Enable:** We partner with Education Departments at district, state, and national levels...to co-create solutions that aim to improve systemic structures and processes and school-level practices.

> **Transform:** We build leadership capacity with the skills and knowledge to develop effective teaching practices...

The unit of change is the **micro-improvement** — "small, achievable actions that create measurable impact over time" ([mantra4change.org/steps](https://www.mantra4change.org/steps/)). Note that ShikshaLokam uses the same micro-improvement framing; per INTENT principle 7, this brain treats the term as Mantra4Change's own house language and does not borrow ShikshaLokam framing into Mantra4Change content.

Sources: [mantra4change.org](https://www.mantra4change.org/), [mantra4change.org/steps](https://www.mantra4change.org/steps/)

## Origin story

Founders Khushboo Awasthi and Santosh More met **Ms. B.S. Vidya, principal of My School in Bengaluru**, and volunteered during a summer camp. That experience seeded the org. STEP launched in **7 pilot schools** in 2013–14; the first full-time hire was **Anoop Erakkil**.

Source: [mantra4change.org/about-us](https://www.mantra4change.org/about-us/)

## Mantra4Change vs Shikshagraha

Per Mantra4Change's own framing, Shikshagraha is a flagship program "under" Mantra4Change — "a people's education movement dedicated to improving 1 million public schools in India by 2030." This differs from ShikshaLokam's framing of the same movement (where ShikshaLokam describes itself as catalyst, not owner). Both orgs co-organise InvokED.

Per **INTENT principle 7**: when this brain produces Mantra4Change content about Shikshagraha, use Mantra4Change's framing. Cross-org alignment on the movement is a real-world fact, but the voice and framing must be Mantra4Change's, not blended.

Source: [mantra4change.org/shikshagraha](https://www.mantra4change.org/shikshagraha/)
