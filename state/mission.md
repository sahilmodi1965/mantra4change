# Mission

This file holds the brain's canonical framing of who Shikshagraha is, what it's solving, why now, how change happens, and what success looks like. Source: Pratik Jain's AI Communication Brief (May 2026).

## Who we are

**Shikshagraha is a people's movement to strengthen all 1 million public schools in India** — so every child, regardless of where they are born, can learn well and be ready for the future.

We are **not an NGO. Not a programme. A movement.**

Founded **November 2023** by **Khushboo Awasthi** — a woman who grew up in Bihar, walked away from a corporate engineering career at WIPRO, and bootstrapped Mantra4Change from personal savings in 2013 with co-founder Santosh More. That organisation grew to reach **275,000 government schools across five states**. The Central Education Department adopted their open-source platform to track education quality nationally. **That decade of work became Shikshagraha.**

Mantra4Change is one of the partner organisations within the movement. This brain serves Mantra4Change's contribution to it — see `INTENT.md` § Who this serves.

## The problem

India has **over 1 million government schools** serving **260 million children**.

Many of these children complete schooling without foundational learning, real confidence, or the skills needed for life, work, or a changing world. **This is not only a resource problem. It is a norm problem.**

Society has slowly stopped feeling responsible for public education. Parents who can afford it flee to private schools. Communities disengage. The system has money — but not momentum. **Shikshagraha provides the momentum.**

Concrete data — see [[scale]] § Problem-side data.

## Why now — the urgency frame

India is in a **once-in-a-generation demographic window**. 25 crore children are in school today. 18 crore youth will enter the workforce by 2030.

**If we act now:** today's primary school children enter adulthood with stronger foundations, digital fluency, scientific temper, and climate awareness. India is better positioned for a just, capable, and sustainable future.

**If we do nothing:** dropout, low literacy, low employability, AI/automation displacement, and climate stress will compound on the children already most at risk. Data: see [[scale]] § "If we do nothing".

**This is not a future problem. It is today's emergency. The window to act is now.**

## What we do (in one line)

**We build the civic infrastructure for school transformation.**

When communities are informed and engaged → they hold schools accountable → teachers are supported → learning improves.

**The district is our unit of change** — where policy, administration, schools, and communities meet.

## The norms we are shifting

This is our theory of change made concrete. Use this table in grant applications, thought leadership, and funder conversations.

| Existing norm | The shift we build | How Shikshagraha responds |
|---|---|---|
| Government schools are only for families with no other options | Government schools every family is proud to choose | Demonstrate vibrant public schools through district transformation |
| Education is the school's responsibility, not the community's | Communities share responsibility for every child's learning | Equip and engage parents, youth, and local leaders |
| Good education means getting good marks | Education that values agency, confidence, curiosity, and real learning | Celebrate stories of learning for life: well-being, application, participation |
| Some children adjusting with less is acceptable — especially girls | Every child deserves the same quality and dignity | Put girls and marginalised voices at the frontlines of leadership and storytelling |
| Teachers must manage everything alone | A culture where teachers lead and grow with support and community | Support teachers through micro-improvement and feedback loops |
| Decisions are taken from the top, far from where children live | Local voices shape decisions and solutions | Co-create with state and district leaders at all levels |
| Education is not a political issue | Public awareness that education is about justice, opportunity and our shared future | Build community demand through civic dialogue and collective action |

## What success looks like

We will know we are succeeding when:

1. **Children are active participants in their own learning** — feedback from children guides teaching; learning becomes personalised and contextual
2. **Communities and teachers lead change** — micro-improvements become normal practice; local solutions inspire district-level action
3. **Education becomes a people's agenda** — parents and youth demand quality; education features in public and political dialogue
4. **Public education earns public pride** — families feel confident choosing government schools; media highlights excellence in public schools
5. **Schools prepare children for the future** — assessments reflect skills, growth, and learnability; children leave school ready for life and work

## Origin story (Mantra4Change → Shikshagraha)

Founders Khushboo Awasthi and Santosh More met **Ms. B.S. Vidya, principal of My School in Bengaluru**, and volunteered during a summer camp. That experience seeded the org in 2013. STEP launched in 7 pilot schools; first full-time hire was Anoop Erakkil. ([mantra4change.org/about-us](https://www.mantra4change.org/about-us/))

Over the next decade, the work reached 275,000 government schools across five states. In November 2023, Khushboo founded Shikshagraha — the movement form of that decade of work.
