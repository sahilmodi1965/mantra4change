# Scale & evidence

The numbers Mantra4Change uses in grant applications, award entries, and donor conversations. From Pratik's AI Communication Brief.

## Movement scale (as of 2025)

- **176,966+** schools part of the movement
- **1,370,606+** micro-improvements in schools
- **354,963+** leaders engaged and growing
- **16 states, 40 districts** active
- **43 momentum partners** across sectors
- **31,861** Chaupals held
- **893,000+** participants in Chaupals
- **2,500+** out-of-school children enrolled
- **800+** children enrolled in school; **900+** dropouts regularised
- **500+** birth certificates and **400+** Aadhaar cards processed — structural-barrier removal
- **100%** enrolment achieved in 50 panchayats

## Third-party evidence — TISS-SCERT 2025

Assessment across **28,989 government schools**:

- Teacher use of Project-Based Learning: **50% → 95%**
- Parent voice in PTMs: **60.8% → 89.4%**
- Students gained **up to two years' learning in one year** (especially Grades 7–8)
- **20% improvement** in 21st-century skills (creativity, collaboration, critical thinking, problem-solving)
- **280,000 girls** reached
- Mixed-gender classrooms building aspiration and confidence

## Problem-side data (use to frame urgency)

For grant and award applications:

- More than **half of Grade 5 students** in government schools cannot read a Grade 2-level text *(ASER 2024)*
- Over **1.1 lakh schools** in India run on a single teacher *(UDISE+ 2023-24)*
- Girls' retention drops from **85.7% (primary) → 47.5% (higher secondary)** *(UDISE+ 2023-24)*
- In 2024 election manifestos, education ranked below other political priorities
- **340–377 districts** fall below the national average in foundational learning *(NAS 2021 / PARAKH RS 2024)*

## "If we do nothing" data (urgency frame)

- Over **14% of children** drop out before completing schooling *(Economic Survey 2024-25)*
- **1 in 4 youth** may continue struggling with reading and numeracy *(Economic Survey 2024-25)*
- Only **47% of graduates** may have basic employability skills *(Economic Survey 2023-24)*

- For award applications, lead with TISS-SCERT third-party validation
- Do not inflate. Pratik's voice rule: **never** "inflated or unverified impact claims"
