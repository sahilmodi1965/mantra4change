# Mantra-STEP Kalaburagi — short-story chapter format

The canonical short-story format Mantra4Change uses to package school transformation stories under the STEP (Systematic Transformation of Education Programme) work. Each chapter is one school, one transformation, one named outcome. Use as **structural template** for any "stories of change" content.

## Format pattern (extract from source)

Each story chapter follows this exact arc:

1. **All-caps title** — names the transformation theme. Example: *"SAMAJ STEPS UP FOR THE SCHOOL"*, *"CLUBS IGNITE CHANGE"*, *"DEMOCRACY 101"*, *"CONFIDENCE UNVEILED"*, *"A DREAM SCHOOL TO ATTEND"*
2. **One-paragraph context** — what was happening in the school before, in plain language with the school name
3. **The trigger / catalyst** — what shifted (an initiative, a recommendation, a person)
4. **The action sequence** — concrete steps the school took
5. **The named outcome** — what changed, with a number when available
6. **One-line theme statement** — closes the chapter with a wider lesson

## Excerpts that demonstrate the rhythm

### "Shaala Prarambhotsava" chapter

> At **Government High School Ingalagi**, the challenge of drop-outs & absenteeism spurred proactive action. With only **70-76** students attending regularly out of **105** enrolled, they seized the opportunity to organize the **"Shaala Prarambhotsava."** Through various initiatives like personalized parent counseling for dropouts, home visits and fun activities, the school attracted students from the vicinity.
>
> This concerted effort resulted in enrollment surge to **130** and with continued efforts of home visits for slow learners, and special classes covering a range of subjects and activities the school achieved complete regularity and **zero absenteeism**. There were **no dropouts** either.
>
> This success underscores the school's dedication to creating an engaging and supportive learning environment, exemplifying the power of proactive measures in tackling educational challenges.

### "Samaj Steps Up" chapter

> Amidst challenges at **Government High School Devikera**, the absence of a functional SDMC committee posed a significant hurdle. Spurred by the recommendations of "Shaala Prarambhotsava" to prioritize parent involvement & SDMC strengthening, the school saw this as an opportunity for transformation. With determination, they reconstituted the **SDMC committee** and within a year, the fruits of their labor became evident through robust collaborations & active participation from SDMC members.
>
> When the school was nominated for upgradation under **PM-SHRI**, there was a requirement for 1.5 acres of land worth 25 lakh rupees. Remarkably, the SDMC stepped up to the challenge, to ensure the criteria were met. Their collective efforts fulfilled the requirements and paved a way for the school's upgradation.
>
> This inspiring journey showcases the **power of community** collaboration and dedication to overcoming obstacles.

### "Confidence Unveiled" chapter (single-student moment)

> At **Government High School Shellagi**, a heartwarming tale unfolded during Pragati hejje. The headmistress proudly shared the journey of their student, Shaila, who struggled with **speech impairment**. However, during the event, Shaila astounded everyone by presenting the Teaching Learning Material so well and with remarkable fluency. Witnessing her confident delivery, her mother was moved to tears.
>
> The headmaster expressed immense joy and optimism for the **program's potential** after witnessing Shaila's transformation. Her newfound confidence not only touched hearts but also ignited hope for what lies ahead.

## Voice rules this exemplifies

- ALWAYS: school named in full ("Government High School Ingalagi" not "the school")
- ALWAYS: number landings ("70-76 of 105," "enrolment surge to 130," "zero absenteeism," "no dropouts")
- ALWAYS: concrete steps before abstract themes ("home visits" before "proactive measures")
- ALWAYS: closing one-line theme ("the power of community" / "confidence" / "engaging and supportive")
- NEVER: vague verbs — every chapter has named actions ("reconstituted," "stepped up," "presented")
- NEVER: programme jargon at the open — initiatives are introduced by quote-marked names with explanation

## When to use this pattern

`mantra4change-write` triggers this pattern for:
- "Draft me a story of change from [school name]"
- "Write a chapter for the coffee table book on [leader name]"
- "Give me a one-page micro-improvement story"
- "Newsletter highlight on [recent school]"
