# Format rules by content type

The length, tone, and key rule for each kind of output. Use this as a checklist before sending.

## Fundraising email

- **Length:** 250–300 words
- **Tone:** Personal, warm, urgent
- **Key rule:** One clear CTA. Open with a human moment, not a statistic.

## Grant application

- **Length:** 200–250 words per section
- **Tone:** Inspirational, provoking, evidence-grounded
- **Key rule:** Lead with the problem, prove with data, close with possibility.

Pair with: problem-side data from [[scale]], norms-shifting frame from [[mission]].

## Award application

- **Length:** Varies
- **Tone:** Human, credible, globally resonant
- **Key rule:** Make judges feel: systemic, unique, inspiring, replicable.

Pair with: [[stories]] — *Mothers of Courage* for cultural awards, Hirehalli for systemic-change awards.

## Social — LinkedIn

- **Length:** No hard limit
- **Tone:** Thoughtful, credible, systemic
- **Key rule:** One strong idea. End with a question or provocation.

## Social — Instagram

- **Length:** Caption only
- **Tone:** Human, emotional, visual
- **Key rule:** Story first. Stat second.
- **Hashtags:** `#Shikshagraha #EducationForAll #CollectiveAction`

## Social — X

- **Length:** Max 150 chars
- **Tone:** Sharp, opinionated, timely
- **Key rule:** One sentence that makes you stop scrolling.

## Case study

- **Length:** 600–750 words
- **Tone:** Grounded, credible, not triumphalist
- **Structure:** Context → Problem → Community action → Change → Quote
- **Key rule:** Always one direct quote from a community member.

Reference structure: Hirehalli in [[stories]] follows this exactly.

## Thought leadership

- **Length:** 800–1,200 words
- **Tone:** First person where appropriate
- **Key rule:** Must take a clear stance. Not balanced to the point of saying nothing.

## Cross-cutting reminders

All formats inherit from [[voice]] (always/never) and should echo at least one of the 5 [[key-messages]].
