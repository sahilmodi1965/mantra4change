# Tough questions — our answers

The questions Shikshagraha gets asked by funders, government, media, and skeptics — and the answers Pratik has signed off on.

Use these verbatim where appropriate. Adapt for length and audience, but do not change the stance.

## Isn't this the government's job?

Public education is led by government — but strengthening it requires collective action. Shikshagraha believes change happens when governments, communities, educators, civil society, and citizens work together. Each plays a different but essential role.

## How do you measure impact?

We track system-level and participation-level shifts: leadership practices, district-level adoption, classroom change, public participation, and sustainability of reforms. We combine quantitative data with field evidence. Our 2025 TISS-SCERT third-party assessment covers 28,989 schools.

Back this with numbers from [[scale]].

## What makes you different from other education NGOs?

Shikshagraha is not a single programme or organisation. It is a collective movement focused on systemic reform. We emphasise district-level transformation, leadership within systems, and building genuine public participation — not delivering services to communities.

Avoid the word "intervention" here — see [[voice]].

## What hasn't worked, and what did you learn?

Sustainable systems change cannot be driven by short-term projects or isolated interventions. Lasting reform requires local ownership, trust-building, alignment across stakeholders, and continuous adaptation. We've learned to go deeper before going wider.

## Are you anti-private schools?

No. Our focus is on strengthening public education because it serves the largest number of children across India — and because no nation can thrive when its public systems are abandoned.

## What does success look like?

Stronger public trust in government education. Deeper collaboration across sectors. Empowered district leadership. Active community participation. Long-term, measurable improvements in how public schools function — and in what children are able to do and become.
