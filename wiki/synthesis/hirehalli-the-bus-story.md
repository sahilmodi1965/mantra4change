## Hirehalli — the bus story

In Hirehalli Gram Panchayat, Karnataka, 69 families across 10 villages had no reliable way to get their children to school. The local Anganwadi was crumbling. Parents were sending 3–5 year olds to distant private schools at great cost. Children were walking dangerous forest paths alone.

For months, requests went unanswered.

Then communities stopped petitioning and started acting. They went directly to the H.D. Kote bus depot manager. He listened. He agreed.

Today, buses run every two hours from 9:30 AM to 6:30 PM on school days. **100 students daily** from Padukote villages. **80+** from Hirehalli villages. **Nearly 1,000 families across 21 villages** now have reliable access to schools and colleges.

The same collective energy revived the Anganwadi. Five children aged 2–3 enrolled, reversing the exodus to private alternatives.

**Sometimes the most profound revolutions begin with the simple resolution that children shouldn't have to risk their lives to learn.**
